## Primary Competitors (Generic AI Consultancies)

### Category: Traditional AI Consulting Firms

**Examples**: Deloitte AI, Accenture AI, PwC AI Practice

**Their Positioning**:
- Large-scale enterprise AI transformation
- Strategy consulting with implementation support
- Industry-specific AI solutions
- Large teams, high fees ($50K-$500K+ engagements)

**Content Strategy**:
- Enterprise-focused case studies
- Industry trend reports
- Whitepapers and research
- Thought leadership from partners

**Our Differentiation**:
- **Target SMBs** (they target Fortune 1000)
- **Systems architecture approach** (they use management consulting frameworks)
- **Platform engineering credibility** (they have MBAs, we have AMD/Intel engineers)
- **Hands-on integration focus** (they create PowerPoints, we architect systems)

**Content Opportunities**:
- "Why SMBs need different AI strategy than enterprises"
- "Platform engineering vs. management consulting for AI"
- "The integration gap large consultancies miss"

---

### Category: AI Tool Vendors (Posing as Strategic Advisors)

**Examples**: HubSpot AI consultants, Salesforce AI advisors

**Their Positioning**:
- Implementation partners for specific AI tools
- "Strategic guidance" that leads to their product
- Tool-first, integration-second approach

**Our Differentiation**:
- **Tool-agnostic** (we recommend what fits, not what we sell)
- **Integration-first** (architecture before tool selection)
- **No vendor lock-in** (neutral systems thinking)

**Content Opportunities**:
- "The hidden cost of vendor-led AI strategy"
- "Tool selection vs. systems architecture"
- "How to evaluate AI consultants: questions to ask"
